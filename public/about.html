Average SAT Scores and Percentages of SAT Test Takers by State

About our Project

Our project will focus on demonstrating the distributio of SAT scores by state across the United Sates. We want to focus on this to see if there are any trends for standardized test scores coast to coast and what the correlations may be. Maybe there will be a trend over time of increasing or decreasing SAT participation. Perhaps different geographic locations may have higher or lower scores, or perhaps timing too will impact SAT scores.

Some terms to know:

  • SAT: The Scholastic Aptitude Test, which is scored out of 1600, measures students' proficiency in high school level mathematics and reading/writing.

    It is most often used in the college admissions process, but in some states like Coloroda it is also a graduation requirement for high school

  • SAT Math Score: The score for the math section of the SAT. There are 58 Questions Total in the math section, broken into two sections of 20 questions and 38 questions
  • SAT EBRW Score: The score for the EBRW section of the SAT, which includes a reading comprehension section and a writing sections, 52 and 44 questions, respectively

Wireframe evaluation:

  • Achievability: We can use plotly for the US map with data per state, but there might not be enough data to do county by county
  • Signifiers: If we can get it to work, being able to click the state to get to a micro view would be very intuitive
  • Simplicity: Really only two forms of graphs in this prototype so not difficult to grasp for user
  • Ease: Again, the signifiers and simplicity should make things pretty easy to navigate

Our final idea:

After swtiching to just SAT scores, we decided we would show the change in scores over years on the macro scale by showing side by side views of SAT participation and scores per state. On the micro scale, we wanted to show the same thing but also break down section scores in bar chart form, which makes it easier to see the progress.
